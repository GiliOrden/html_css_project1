The first intifada was a mostly violent uprising, weakened significantly after the Gulf War, and officially ended with the signing of the Oslo Accords in 1993. During it, the Palestinian residents of Judea and Samaria and the Gaza Strip revolted against the State of Israel. The intifada was sometimes called the "stone war", because at the beginning the Palestinians used stones and other improvised weapons. The goals of the intifada were divided into two: Immediate goals related to Judea, Samaria and Gaza themselves. and long-term policy goals.
