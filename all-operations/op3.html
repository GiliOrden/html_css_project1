The second intifada was a violent Palestinian uprising against the State of Israel, which began at the end of September 2000. It broke out because of the events of the Wall Tunnel and the events of Nakba Day 2000. Terrorist operations and guerrilla warfare became its main expression. A distinct feature of the Palestinian activity in this struggle was the widespread use of suicide attacks against the citizens of the State of Israel, which began as early as October 2000, about a month after it began and increased as it continued. The second intifada almost completely overturned the Oslo agreement of 1993. The construction of the separation fence and the death of Yasser Arafat at the end of 2004 led to the decline of the wave of terrorism in Judea and Samaria.
