Operation Cast Lead was a large-scale military operation led by the IDF in the Gaza Strip, following the firing of rockets from the Gaza Strip at civilians and settlements in the western Negev. Three objectives were defined for the operation: the first - to create a security calm that would survive over time, the second - to prevent Hamas from arming itself, and the third - Return the kidnapped soldier Gilad Shalit. This goal was set only towards the end of the operation. On January 17, after 22 days of fighting, Israel announced a unilateral ceasefire, which came into effect the next day at 2 a.m., and its forces began to gradually withdraw from the Strip and finished their departure on January 21.
